Project 4 - Photonics for Confidential Computing

Posted on: November 5, 2024

Category: Tech | Year: 2024

Problem Statement

    Data privacy is the most critical concern for national security, AI, healthcare, and more.

    Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) allows computation on encrypted data without requiring the secret key -> privacy preserving

    However, FHE suffer needs huge compute resource making it impractical -> 10,000x

    Researchers has been exploring algorithmic and hardware optimization to accelerate

    However, memory bandwidth is the deal breaker due to irregular access pattern

Solution

    State-of-the-art FHE acceleration techniques utilize wafer-scale solutions requiring ASIC chiplets to communicate with HBMs in 3D.

    Photonics have proven to be extremely effective in communicating between compute and memory cores in deep neural networks.

    We are proposing photonic acceleration to replace the complex MUX/interconnections to realize a scalable and configurable network

    Work in progress with AIM photonics using Synopsys tools to make FHE a reality.

    10s Gbps -> 10s/100s Tbps + Low power + compact and scalable solution
