Project 3 - Secure Heterogeneous Integration

Posted on: November 5, 2024

Category: Tech | Year: 2024

Problem Statement

    Heterogeneous integration (HI) involves combining diverse chiplets, each sourced from potentially untrusted vendors, into a single package  realize “more than Moore” concept

    Authentication mechanisms are essential to verify the trustworthiness of these chiplets throughout their lifecycle, from manufacturing to deployment.

    The presence of an active interposer and the combination of different components may cause varied power consumption or EM characteristics, leading to multifold side-channel and tampering attacks.

    Manufacturing test requires - (i) pre-integration chiplet testing, (ii) sharing sensitive test data, and (iii) package testing.

Solution

    Authenticate using secure multi-party computation

    Each of chiplets engage in computing the outcome of a function without sharing the secret -> verifies authenticity

    Only authenticated chiplets engage in DFT and function

    Large system-on-chips require splitting or partitioning to smaller chiplets to be fabricated in advanced technology nodes, e.g., 2nm to maintain the yield.

    We are developing secure splitting of intellectual properties without revealing the internal secrets in the interposer level using graph partition algorithm.
